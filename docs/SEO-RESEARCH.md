# LME Building Contractors — SEO & AI-Search Research

> Research deliverable (June 2026). Nothing here is implemented on the site yet — this is
> the strategy/keyword/FAQ/AEO research to work from. Cost figures are 2026 UK ranges and
> are **indicative**; Lincolnshire (East Midlands) typically sits **at or below the national
> average** and well under London/South-East figures — confirm your own numbers before publishing.

## 1. Strategy in one paragraph

LME is a **Sleaford-based building contractor** serving a ~25-mile radius of Lincolnshire.
For a local trade business, the winning play is **local SEO**: ranking for
**"[service] + [town]"** searches and **"near me"** searches, backed by a fully optimised
**Google Business Profile (GBP)**, consistent **NAP** citations, **reviews**, and
**structured data**. National head terms ("house extension cost") are high-volume but
brutally competitive and low-intent for a local builder — we target them only through
**helpful long-form/FAQ content** (which also feeds AI search), not as primary money pages.
The money pages are **service pages** and **service-×-location landing pages**.

**Three intent tiers we target:**
| Tier | Example | Page that should win it |
|------|---------|------------------------|
| Transactional / local | "house extension builders sleaford" | Service + location landing page / Services page |
| Commercial investigation | "how much does a single storey extension cost", "best builders in Lincoln" | Service page + FAQ + cost guide content |
| Informational (feeds AI search) | "do I need planning permission for an extension" | Blog/FAQ articles with schema |

## 4. FAQ content (ready to use) — optimised for featured snippets & AI answers

**Format rule for AI search:** lead with a **direct, ~40–55 word answer** in plain
conversational language, then add detail. Mark up with **FAQPage schema**. Use the real
question as the visible heading.

### Home Extensions page FAQ
1. **How much does a house extension cost in Lincolnshire?**
   As a guide, a single-storey extension typically costs around £1,800–£2,800 per m² in
   Lincolnshire (so roughly £40,000–£70,000 for a 20m² build), and a two-storey extension
   often works out cheaper per m² as foundations and roof are shared. Every project is quoted
   individually after a free site visit. *(VAT at 20% applies to most extension work.)*
2. **Do I need planning permission for an extension?**
   Often not — many single-storey rear extensions fall under Permitted Development (up to 4m
   for a detached house, 3m for others, within size limits). You'll usually still need Building
   Regulations approval. Conservation areas and listed buildings have stricter rules. We handle
   drawings, Building Control and any planning application for you.
3. **How long does an extension take to build?**
   On site, a single-storey extension usually takes 8–16 weeks and a two-storey 14–24 weeks.
   Allow extra lead time for design, drawings and any planning approval (often 8–12 weeks)
   before work starts. We agree a clear programme up front and keep you updated at every stage.
4. **What's included in your extension service?**
   Everything from concept design and structural calculations to Building Regulations sign-off,
   groundworks, steelwork, roofing, plastering, electrics, plumbing and full internal finishing —
   so you have a single, accountable point of contact from start to handover.
5. **Will an extension add value to my home?**
   A well-built extension typically adds both space and resale value, especially kitchen-diner
   and additional-bedroom extensions. We're happy to advise on the best layout for your budget
   and how it fits your home and street.

### Renovations page FAQ
1. **How much does a full house renovation cost?**
   A full renovation in Lincolnshire typically runs £1,200–£2,000 per m² depending on
   specification (a cosmetic refresh costs far less than a full rewire, re-plumb and
   re-configuration). Period and listed properties usually cost 20–40% more due to specialist
   materials and methods. We provide a clear, itemised quote after a site visit.
2. **Do you renovate period and listed properties?**
   Yes. We sympathetically restore period and listed homes using appropriate materials such as
   lime mortar and reclaimed stone, and we manage any listed building consent required. Our
   Sleaford cottage refurbishment is a good example.
3. **Can you remove internal/load-bearing walls for open-plan living?**
   Yes — structural alterations like removing load-bearing walls and knock-throughs are core to
   our renovation work. We arrange the structural calculations, steelwork and Building Control
   sign-off as part of the job.
4. **Should I budget a contingency?**
   Yes — for renovations we recommend a 10–15% contingency for hidden issues (older wiring,
   damp, structural surprises), which is normal for refurbishment projects. We flag risks early
   and keep you informed so there are no surprises.

### New Builds page FAQ
1. **How much does it cost to build a new house per square metre?**
   In 2026, new build costs in the UK generally range from about £1,800–£3,000 per m² depending
   on specification, with Lincolnshire toward the lower-to-mid end versus London. That's the
   build only — land, fees and VAT are separate. We'll give a tailored estimate for your plot
   and plans.
2. **Do I pay VAT on a new build?**
   New build homes are generally zero-rated for VAT — a significant saving versus extensions and
   renovations, which are usually standard-rated at 20%. We'll advise on how this applies to
   your project.
3. **Do you offer self-build support, or only turnkey builds?**
   Both. We deliver complete turnkey new homes and also support self-builders with as much or as
   little of the build as you need, working from your plans or helping develop them.

### Loft Conversions page FAQ
1. **How much does a loft conversion cost?**
   A dormer loft conversion typically costs around £30,000–£55,000 in 2026 depending on type and
   size (Velux/rooflight conversions are cheaper; mansard conversions cost more). We quote each
   loft individually after assessing head height and structure.
2. **Do I need planning permission for a loft conversion?**
   Most rear dormers and rooflight conversions fall under Permitted Development (within volume
   limits — 40m³ terraces, 50m³ semis/detached) and don't need planning permission, unless
   you're in a conservation area or the dormer faces the road. Building Regulations always apply.
3. **How long does a loft conversion take?**
   Typically 6–10 weeks on site, plus lead time for design, structural calculations and any
   approvals beforehand.

### About / Contact / sitewide FAQ (trust questions — strong for AI search)
1. **Are you insured and accredited?**
   Yes — we carry full public liability and employer's liability insurance and hold recognised
   industry accreditations (Checkatrade, TrustMark, FMB, Gas Safe, NHBC, Which? Trusted Trader).
   Documentation is available on request.
2. **Do you offer a guarantee?**
   Yes — all our work is backed by a 10-year workmanship guarantee for genuine peace of mind
   long after handover.
3. **What areas do you cover?**
   We're based in Sleaford and cover roughly a 25-mile radius across Lincolnshire, including
   Lincoln, Grantham, Boston, Spalding, Bourne, Newark and surrounding villages. If your town
   isn't listed, get in touch — we may still be able to help.
4. **Do you provide free quotes?**
   Yes — every project starts with a free, no-obligation site visit and consultation, followed
   by a clear, itemised written quotation.
5. **Do you handle planning permission and building control?**
   Yes — we manage architectural drawings, structural calculations, Building Regulations and any
   planning applications, so you have a single point of contact throughout.
6. **What questions should I ask a builder before hiring?**
   Ask for proof of insurance, examples of similar past work and references, trade-body
   membership (e.g. FMB), a written itemised quote, a clear programme tied to payment stages,
   and what guarantee they offer. We're happy to provide all of these up front.

## 5. Showing up in AI search (AEO / GEO) — what to implement

AI search (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) "surfaces fewer businesses with
higher specificity, favouring those with rich, trustworthy, well-structured online presences."
Structured data + consistent citations + reviews are the levers. Priorities:

### 5a. Structured data (schema.org JSON-LD) — highest leverage
- **LocalBusiness → `HomeAndConstructionBusiness` / `GeneralContractor`** on the homepage:
  legal name, logo, image, full address (NAP), phone, email, `geo` coordinates, `areaServed`
  (list the towns), `openingHoursSpecification`, `priceRange`, `url`, `sameAs` (GBP, Checkatrade,
  Facebook, FMB profile, etc.).
- **`Service`** schema on each service page (serviceType, provider, areaServed, description).
- **`FAQPage`** schema wrapping every FAQ block (this is what gets you into AI answers and
  "People also ask").
- **`AggregateRating` / `Review`** schema for testimonials (the ★4.9 / 127 reviews) — only mark
  up reviews you can substantiate (Google requires they be genuine and visible on the page).
- **`BreadcrumbList`** once real URLs exist.
- **`Organization`** + **`WebSite`** (with `sitelinks searchbox` optional).
- Optionally **`CreativeWork`/`Project`** style markup for portfolio case studies.

### 5b. Google Business Profile (the #1 local + AI-local signal)
- Claim/verify; choose primary category **"General contractor"** plus relevant secondary
  categories (e.g. "Construction company", "Home builder", "Building restoration service").
- Fill **every** field: description, services (with individual descriptions matching the
  keyword clusters above), service area (the towns), hours, attributes, website, photos.
- Add **real project photos** regularly; collect and **respond to every review**.
- Post updates/offers (the "£500 off / free consultation" offer is ideal GBP post material).

### 5c. NAP consistency + citations
- Use **one identical** business name, address, phone everywhere.
- Build/clean citations on: **Checkatrade, FMB Find-a-Builder, TrustMark, Which? Trusted Trader,
  Gas Safe, NHBC, Yell, MyBuilder, Bark, Houzz, Facebook, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect**.
  (Quality citations are reportedly the #4 ranking factor for appearing in AI responses.)

### 5d. Content written the way people (and AI) ask
- **Answer-first**: each page/section opens with a concise, direct answer to its target question.
- Use the **real question as the heading** (H2/H3) and conversational phrasing.
- Cover **cost, time, planning, process, guarantee** for every service — these are the exact
  things buyers (and AI) ask.
- Publish **cost guides & planning guides** ("House Extension Cost in Lincolnshire 2026",
  "Do I Need Planning Permission for an Extension in Lincolnshire?") — these rank for the
  informational long-tails and get cited by AI.

### 5e. E-E-A-T / trust signals
- Named team with experience, real photos, named author on guides.
- Display accreditations (done), insurance statement, company registration, guarantee.
- Genuine reviews on-site + linked to Google/Checkatrade.
- Real project case studies with location, scope, before/after, and outcomes.

### 5f. Technical SEO checklist
- **Unique `<title>` (~55–60 chars) and meta description (~150–160 chars) per page**, keyword-
  + location-led (examples below).
- One clear **H1 per page**; logical H2/H3 with keywords.
- **Descriptive image alt text** (e.g. "single storey rear extension in Sleaford") — already
  partly in place.
- **`sitemap.xml`** + **`robots.txt`** + canonical tags.
- Fast, mobile-first, accessible (already strong) — Core Web Vitals matter for ranking.
- Internal linking between services ↔ locations ↔ projects ↔ guides.
- Real, crawlable URLs (see §3 note about the hash router).
